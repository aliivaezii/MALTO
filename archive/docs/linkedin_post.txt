I recently took part in the MALTO Recruitment Hackathon organized by the MALTO team
at Politecnico di Torino. The challenge was to build a classifier that distinguishes
human-written text from text generated by five different AI models -- DeepSeek, Grok,
Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT.

I started with TF-IDF baselines and classical ML models, then moved to fine-tuning
DeBERTa-v3-base with 5-fold cross-validation and Focal Loss to handle the heavy class
imbalance (up to 19:1 ratio). The final solution blends the transformer with classical
models through optimized weighted soft voting, reaching a Macro F1 of 0.92170 on the
public leaderboard.

The hardest part was separating DeepSeek and Grok from each other -- two minority classes
that together represent only 10 percent of the data and share similar writing patterns.

The full pipeline (8 Jupyter notebooks covering EDA, feature engineering, transformer
training, and ensemble optimization) is open-sourced on my GitHub.
